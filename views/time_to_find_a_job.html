Time to find a job

I am looking for a job.

Employers typically have HR departments with staff whose only jobs is to build and maintain these hiring pipelines. Those that don't seem increasingly to be outsourcing the process to third party recruitment agencies.

Honestly, I feel outgunned by all of this. I'm just one person. I don't like talking about myself. I am deeply sceptical of the kind of language that seems to be used when advertising ones professional credentials. I feel like a bird of paradise. Required to perform an elaborate courtship display involving song, dance, and hyperspecific physical displays. It's just that I have no instinct for it. I actively dislike the tacit rules of engagement. Some of it seems to fly in the face of what employers purportedley want.

Surely I can level the playing field somewhat by using my knowlege of R to make the search more efficient, more effective, and more enjoyable. I can gain a little more insight into the game itself. I'm telling employers that I'm a good researcher. That I know a lot about data and maths and stuff. It would almost be weird if I didn't plug it all into R and turn the optimization crank.

I have some basic search parameters. I'm not really bothered about public or private sector. The main restrictions are that I want jobs that fit my skillset and my interests and they must be located in the UK/Europe.

The flow of the project will be to cast a wide net for potential jobs by scraping a number of job sites based on some keywords. I'll do a bit of manual searching to build up an idea of what keywords give me what I think are relevant results. I mean, job sites do spend a lot of time tailoring their algorithms to serve you jobs that might be a good fit based on your searches. Although I do want to maintain a sort of ``open'' or ``exploratory'' search strategy rather than being pushed down particular routes by recommendation algorithms.

I fully anticipate having to put a fair amount of ``manual'' work into each application. I'd like to be able to do some analysis of the aggregate data to optimize the work that I have to do. For instance, I'd like to make sure that I prioritize applications that close soon To maximise the number of ``shots'' I can take. I'd also like to formulate some sense of how closely aligned my interests and skills are to the job itself while also developing some understanding something of the qualitative nature of the workplace in question. For example, there are some job postings where a reading of the description provides a fairly solid if not exact outline of what the job entails. That is, they communicate the nature of the work with both precision and accuracy. Interestingly, job listings for tradespeople typically takes this form. ``Wanted: qualified plumber/gasfitters''. It could fit on a postage stamp. There is, to my mind, another kind of job posting that communicates with neither accuracy or precision. Solow and Orwell turn in their graves. infantilising. coporatese word salad. vague about the job (imprecise). Unfortunately I can't speak to the accuracy but I maintain a very powerful suspicion that imprecision can be read as a strong signal that there is no small amount of gold-plating involved.

It should be entertaining to be able to form some insights into the way that job postings are written. My impression is that it is a specific species of prose that is highly sylised and idiomatic. I'm just fascinated by the language used the cliches and dead metaphors. In many ways, it's a shame Orwell wasn't around to see the development of NLP.

R should then output application documents, typically a CV and covering letter, tailored to the job in question. I can do this by automatically generating text files that can be used to prompt ChatGPT. Such a prompt will need to have information about me and my background and information about the desired qualities for the advertised role.

Again, I suspect that I'll need to spend some time massaging the ChatGPT output for a final pass. Let's be real LLMs are here to stay. I'm hoping that the LLM will make me sound like more of a normal, appealing candidate and I can pare back anything that I think is too egregious.
